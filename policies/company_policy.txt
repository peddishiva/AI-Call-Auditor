Customer Support Practice Policy 
1. Purpose

This policy defines the mandatory standards, ethical principles, and operational procedures governing customer support interactions.
Its purpose is to ensure consistent, secure, respectful, and effective customer service across all communication channels.

2. Applicability

This policy applies to:

All customer support personnel (inbound, outbound, chat, email, and digital support)

All employment types (permanent, temporary, contractual, probationary)

All customer interaction channels (voice, text, email, messaging, automated systems)

Local laws, regulations, or contractual obligations override this policy where stricter.

3. Core Principles

All customer interactions must adhere to the following principles:

Customer-centricity

Professionalism

Honesty and transparency

Respect and non-discrimination

Confidentiality and data protection

Accountability and traceability

4. Definitions

Agent / CCE: A person authorised to interact with customers on behalf of the organisation

Customer: Any individual or entity receiving support

Interaction: Any customer contact via approved channels

Ticket / Case: A unique system-generated record of an interaction

Escalation: Transfer of responsibility to a higher support level

5. Communication Standards
5.1 Tone and Language

Must be professional, calm, polite, and empathetic

Must avoid slang, profanity, sarcasm, or hostile language

Must be clear, concise, and understandable

5.2 Conduct

Actively listen before responding

Acknowledge customer concerns

Avoid interrupting

Maintain neutrality and composure at all times

6. Identification and Verification
6.1 Mandatory Verification

Before discussing any account-specific or sensitive information:

Verify customer identity using approved verification attributes

Verification must be completed successfully before proceeding

6.2 Prohibited Actions

Discussing account details without verification

Accessing customer data without a legitimate business purpose

7. Problem Handling and Resolution
7.1 Issue Identification

Confirm understanding of the issue through summarisation or clarification

Use approved knowledge bases and procedures

7.2 Resolution Rules

Attempt first-contact resolution where feasible

If resolution exceeds predefined time or complexity thresholds, escalate

7.3 Escalation

Escalation must follow defined hierarchy and procedures

Ownership of the issue must remain clear until resolution

8. Documentation and Ticketing

Every interaction must be documented

Each interaction must be associated with a unique ticket or case ID

Records must include:

Issue description

Actions taken

Resolution or next steps

Responsible party

9. Monitoring and Quality Assurance

Interactions may be monitored or recorded for:

Training

Quality assurance

Compliance

Dispute resolution

Monitoring must comply with applicable laws and privacy requirements

10. Closing Standards

Before ending an interaction:

Confirm whether additional assistance is required

Close politely and professionally

Ensure the customer is informed of next steps if unresolved

11. Expected Behaviour

Agents must:

Treat all customers equally and respectfully

Follow organisational policies and legal requirements

Protect customer data and confidentiality

Take responsibility for assigned tasks

Act in good faith and integrity

12. Unacceptable Behaviour

The following are prohibited:

Harassment, discrimination, or abuse

Misrepresentation of information

Breach of confidentiality or data misuse

Failure to follow verification or escalation procedures

Falsification or omission of records

13. Violation Classification

Zero Tolerance Violations

Abuse, harassment, discrimination

Intentional data breaches

Critical Violations

Failure to verify identity

Unauthorised disclosure of information

Minor Violations

Incomplete documentation

Procedural deviations without customer harm

14. Consequences

Violations may result in:

Coaching or retraining

Formal warnings

Suspension

Termination
Severity and recurrence determine action.

15. Training and Continuous Improvement

Mandatory onboarding and periodic refresher training

Regular performance and compliance reviews

Policy updates communicated promptly

16. Policy Governance

This policy is subject to periodic review

Amendments may be made due to legal, operational, or strategic changes

All personnel are responsible for compliance
